Our Mission

This website exists to help Nigerian Ethereum holders find safe, fast, and cost-effective platforms for converting ETH to Nigerian Naira. Nigeria has one of the most active cryptocurrency markets in Africa, with millions of users buying and selling digital assets each year. Yet for many Nigerians — particularly those outside major financial centres — navigating the range of platforms available is difficult, and the consequences of choosing the wrong platform can be significant: slow payouts, poor exchange rates, hidden fees, or security risks.

Our mission is straightforward: provide honest, independently produced information that helps Nigerian ETH sellers make better-informed decisions. We focus exclusively on the Nigerian market, and every platform we review is evaluated from the perspective of a Nigerian user seeking to convert Ethereum to Naira and receive funds in a local bank account.

Who We Are

We are a small editorial team with backgrounds in cryptocurrency, personal finance journalism, and the Nigerian fintech sector. We do not disclose individual team member names — our credibility is established through the quality and accuracy of our research, not personal branding. We are based in Nigeria and have direct experience using the platforms we review.

Our team has been active in the Nigerian crypto market since 2018, through the CBN directive of 2021, the subsequent shift to P2P platforms, and the partial regulatory reversal of 2023. This continuity means we understand the practical realities of selling crypto in Nigeria in ways that purely desk-based research cannot capture.

How We Work

We research each platform by reviewing its official documentation, fee schedules, and terms of service. Where possible, we conduct or collect first-hand test transactions to verify claimed exchange rates, payout speeds, and bank compatibility. We cross-reference platform claims against community reports from Nigerian users on forums, social media, and direct user feedback.

Rankings are updated periodically — typically when a platform makes significant changes to its fee structure, rate competitiveness, or feature set; when a new platform launches that is relevant to Nigerian ETH sellers; or when regulatory changes affect the operating environment.

Editorial Independence

No platform reviewed on this site has paid for its ranking position. We do not accept payment, equity stakes, advertising agreements, or any other form of compensation in exchange for ranking placement or editorial coverage. We have no affiliate relationships with any platform reviewed — we do not earn commission when users click through to a platform's website or create an account.

Our rankings reflect our honest assessment of each platform's suitability for Nigerian Ethereum sellers based on the criteria described in our Editorial Policy.
